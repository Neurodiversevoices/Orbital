## PURPOSE

These State Law Addenda supplement and modify the Master Corporate Documents of [CORPORATION NAME], Inc. to ensure compliance with the laws of each U.S. state and the District of Columbia. # ALABAMA

## Indemnification
Alabama follows the Model Business Corporation Act. A corporation may indemnify directors and officers against liability arising from their corporate roles if they acted in good faith and reasonably believed their conduct was in the corporation's best interest. Ala. Code § 10A-2A-8.50 et seq.

## Director/Officer Standards
Directors must act in good faith, with the care an ordinarily prudent person would use, and in a manner reasonably believed to be in the corporation's best interest. Ala. Code § 10A-2A-8.30.

## Non-Competition
Alabama enforces reasonable non-competition agreements. Courts apply reasonableness analysis to scope, duration, and geographic area. No specific statutory restrictions.

## Invention Assignment
No statutory exception for employee inventions created on personal time.

---

# ALASKA

## Indemnification
Alaska permits indemnification of directors and officers who acted in good faith and reasonably believed their conduct was lawful and in the corporation's best interest. AS § 10.06.490.

## Director/Officer Standards
Directors owe duties of care and loyalty. Business judgment rule protects good faith decisions. AS § 10.06.450.

## Non-Competition
Alaska courts enforce reasonable non-compete agreements. No blanket prohibition. Reasonableness factors include duration, geographic scope, and protectable business interest.

## Invention Assignment
No statutory exception for employee inventions.

# ARIZONA

## Indemnification
Arizona permits broad indemnification under A.R.S. § 10-851 et seq. Mandatory indemnification required when director/officer is wholly successful on the merits.

## Director/Officer Standards
Directors must act in good faith, with ordinary care, and in a manner reasonably believed to be in the corporation's best interest. A.R.S. § 10-830.

## Non-Competition
Arizona enforces reasonable non-competes. A.R.S. § 23-494 restricts non-competes for low-wage workers (earning less than median wage).

## Invention Assignment
**MANDATORY NOTICE:** Arizona law (A.R.S. § 44-325) provides that an employee cannot be required to assign an invention developed entirely on the employee's own time without using employer equipment, supplies, facilities, or trade secrets, unless the invention relates to the employer's business or results from work performed for the employer.

# CALIFORNIA

## Indemnification
California permits indemnification under Corp. Code § 317. Agents who acted in good faith may be indemnified. Mandatory indemnification for successful defense.

## Director/Officer Standards
Directors owe duty of care and loyalty. Business judgment rule applies. Corp. Code § 309.

## Non-Competition
**CRITICAL:** California Business & Professions Code § 16600 voids non-competition agreements except in limited sale-of-business contexts. Non-compete provisions in Article 6 of the Master Documents are UNENFORCEABLE in California.

**MANDATORY REVISION:** For California employees and contractors:
- Section 6.1 (Non-Competition Covenant) does not apply
- Section 6.2 (Definition of Competing Business) does not apply
- Section 6.3 (Geographic Scope) does not apply
- Non-solicitation of employees remains enforceable under narrow circumstances

## Invention Assignment
**MANDATORY NOTICE (Cal. Lab. Code § 2870):** California law requires that an employment agreement that requires assignment of inventions must contain the following notice:

> "This Agreement does not apply to an invention for which no equipment, supplies, facilities, or trade secret information of the Company was used and which was developed entirely on the employee's own time, unless (a) the invention relates (i) to the business of the Company, or (ii) to the Company's actual or demonstrably anticipated research or development, or (b) the invention results from any work performed by the employee for the Company."

# COLORADO

## Indemnification

## Director/Officer Standards

## Non-Competition
**CRITICAL:** C.R.S. § 8-2-113 significantly restricts non-competes. Non-competes are void except for:
1. Executive and management personnel
2. Staff with access to trade secrets

## Invention Assignment
**MANDATORY NOTICE (C.R.S. § 8-2-113(4)):** Colorado requires notice that the invention assignment does not apply to inventions for which no company equipment, supplies, facilities, or trade secret information was used and developed entirely on personal time, unless related to company business.

# DELAWARE

## Indemnification
**MANDATORY LANGUAGE:** Delaware General Corporation Law § 145 governs indemnification. Delaware permits broad indemnification provisions.

> The Corporation shall indemnify any person who was or is a party or is threatened to be made a party to any threatened, pending or completed action, suit or proceeding, whether civil, criminal, administrative or investigative, by reason of the fact that the person is or was a director or officer of the Corporation, to the fullest extent permitted by the Delaware General Corporation Law.

## Director/Officer Standards
Directors owe fiduciary duties of care and loyalty. Business judgment rule provides protection for informed, good faith decisions. 8 Del. C. § 141(e).

## Exculpation
**Delaware permits exculpation from monetary liability for breach of duty of care (not loyalty).** Consider adding to Certificate of Incorporation under 8 Del. C. § 102(b)(7).

## Non-Competition
Delaware enforces reasonable non-compete agreements. Blue-pencil doctrine may be applied to modify overbroad provisions.

## Invention Assignment
**MANDATORY NOTICE (19 Del. C. § 805):** Delaware law provides that an employee cannot be required to assign rights to an invention for which no company equipment, supplies, facilities, or trade secrets were used and which was developed entirely on the employee's own time, unless it relates to the company's business or results from company work.

Va. Code § 13.1-696 et seq. permits indemnification following MBCA.

## Director/Officer Standards
Directors must act in good faith, with ordinary care, and in the corporation's best interest.

## Non-Competition
**IMPORTANT:** Va. Code § 40.1-28.7:8 prohibits non-competes with low-wage employees (earning less than weekly average wage).

**MANDATORY REVISION:** Virginia non-competes unenforceable against low-wage employees.

## Invention Assignment
No statutory exception for employee inventions.

# WASHINGTON

## Indemnification
RCW § 23B.08.510 et seq. permits indemnification following MBCA.

## Director/Officer Standards
Directors must act in good faith, with ordinary care, and in the corporation's best interest.

## Non-Competition
**CRITICAL:** RCW § 49.62.020 restricts non-competes:
- Employees must earn above $116,593.18 (adjusted annually for inflation)
- Independent contractors must earn above $291,482.95 (adjusted annually)
- 18-month maximum duration
- Cannot restrict sideline business that doesn't compete

**MANDATORY REVISION:** Washington non-competes subject to compensation thresholds and 18-month duration limit.

## Invention Assignment
**MANDATORY NOTICE (RCW § 49.44.140):** Washington law provides that employees cannot be required to assign inventions developed entirely on personal time without using company resources, unless related to company business.

# WISCONSIN

## Indemnification
Wis. Stat. § 180.0850 et seq. permits indemnification following MBCA.

## Director/Officer Standards
Directors must act in good faith, with ordinary care, and in the corporation's best interest.

## Non-Competition
Wis. Stat. § 103.465 governs. Non-compete must be necessary to protect employer, impose only reasonably necessary restrictions, and not be harsh or oppressive.

## Invention Assignment
**MANDATORY NOTICE (Wis. Stat. § 109.07):** Wisconsin law provides that employees cannot be required to assign inventions developed entirely on personal time without using company resources, unless related to company business.
