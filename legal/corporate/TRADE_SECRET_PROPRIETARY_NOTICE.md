## SECTION 6 — CONFIDENTIALITY OBLIGATIONS

### 6.1 Recipient Obligations
Any person or entity receiving access to the Company's trade secrets or confidential information agrees to:

1. Maintain strict confidentiality of all such information
2. Use such information only for authorized purposes
3. Not disclose such information to any third party without prior written authorization
4. Protect such information using at least the same degree of care used to protect their own confidential information
5. Return or destroy all such information upon request or termination of authorization
6. Promptly notify the Company of any unauthorized access or disclosure

### 6.2 Authorized Disclosures
Authorized disclosure of confidential information may be made only:

1. To employees, contractors, or agents with a need to know, who are bound by confidentiality obligations
2. Pursuant to a valid written agreement with the Company
3. As required by law, court order, or government regulation, provided prior notice is given to the Company where permitted

## SECTION 7 — PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES

### 7.1 Prohibited Conduct
The following activities are strictly prohibited without prior written authorization from the Company:

1. **Copying or Reproduction**
   - Copying, reproducing, or duplicating any proprietary materials
   - Creating derivative works based on proprietary materials
   - Photographing, recording, or capturing proprietary materials

2. **Reverse Engineering**
   - Reverse engineering, decompiling, or disassembling any software
   - Attempting to derive source code, algorithms, or data structures
   - Analyzing or deconstructing products or services to discover trade secrets

3. **Unauthorized Access**
   - Accessing systems, networks, or data without authorization
   - Circumventing security measures or access controls
   - Using unauthorized credentials or methods to gain access

4. **Misappropriation**
   - Using trade secrets for personal gain or competitive advantage
   - Disclosing trade secrets to competitors or unauthorized parties
   - Inducing others to disclose or misappropriate trade secrets

5. **Competitive Use**
   - Using proprietary information to develop competing products or services
   - Assisting competitors with proprietary information
   - Benchmarking or comparative analysis using proprietary information
