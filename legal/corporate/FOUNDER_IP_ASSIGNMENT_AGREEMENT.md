## ARTICLE 1 — DEFINITIONS

### 1.1 "Company Intellectual Property"
means all Inventions, Developments, works of authorship, designs, know-how, ideas, concepts, discoveries, improvements, trade secrets, and other intellectual property, whether or not patentable or copyrightable, that:

(a) relate to the Company's actual or anticipated business, research, or development;

(b) result from any work performed by the Founder for or on behalf of the Company;

(c) are developed using the Company's equipment, supplies, facilities, or Confidential Information; or

(d) are otherwise developed in the scope of the Founder's engagement with the Company.

### 1.2 "Confidential Information"
means any and all non-public information, in any form, relating to the Company or its business, including but not limited to:

(a) trade secrets, inventions, ideas, processes, formulas, algorithms, source code, object code, data, programs, and other works of authorship;

(b) product plans, designs, specifications, drawings, prototypes, and development roadmaps;

(c) customer lists, supplier lists, vendor information, pricing information, and cost data;

(d) business plans, marketing plans, financial information, projections, and strategies;

(e) research and development activities, methodologies, and results;

(f) personnel information, organizational structures, and compensation data;

(g) any information designated as confidential or that would reasonably be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information or circumstances of disclosure.

### 1.3 "Developments"
means any invention, discovery, idea, concept, design, improvement, modification, derivative work, know-how, process, technique, algorithm, software, database, trade secret, or other intellectual property, whether or not patentable or copyrightable.

### 1.4 "Inventions"
means any invention, discovery, idea, concept, improvement, process, machine, manufacture, composition of matter, or design, whether or not patentable.

### 1.5 "Prior Inventions"
means all Inventions and Developments that were made, conceived, reduced to practice, or owned by the Founder prior to the Founder's engagement with the Company and that are not assigned to the Company under this Agreement, as listed in Exhibit A.

## ARTICLE 2 — ASSIGNMENT OF INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### 2.1 Assignment of Prior Inventions (if applicable)
The Founder hereby irrevocably assigns, transfers, and conveys to the Company, and the Company hereby accepts, all right, title, and interest in and to the Prior Inventions listed in Exhibit B as "Assigned Prior Inventions," including all patents, copyrights, trade secrets, and other intellectual property rights therein.

### 2.2 Assignment of Company Intellectual Property
The Founder hereby irrevocably assigns, transfers, and conveys to the Company, and the Company hereby accepts, all right, title, and interest in and to all Company Intellectual Property, including but not limited to:

(a) all Inventions and Developments conceived, created, developed, made, or reduced to practice by the Founder, alone or with others, during the Founder's engagement with the Company;

(b) all patents, patent applications, and rights to apply for patents in any country;

(c) all copyrights, mask works, and rights in works of authorship;

(d) all trade secrets and rights in Confidential Information;

(e) all trademarks, trade names, service marks, and trade dress associated with Company Intellectual Property;

(f) all rights to sue for past, present, and future infringement or misappropriation;

(g) all other intellectual property rights of any kind or nature.

### 2.3 Work Made for Hire
The Founder agrees that all works of authorship created by the Founder within the scope of the Founder's engagement with the Company shall be considered "works made for hire" as defined by the United States Copyright Act. To the extent any such work is not a work made for hire, the Founder hereby assigns all right, title, and interest in such work to the Company.

### 2.4 Moral Rights
To the extent permitted by applicable law, the Founder hereby waives and agrees not to assert any "moral rights" or similar rights in any Company Intellectual Property.

### 2.5 Further Assurances
The Founder agrees to execute and deliver all documents, applications, assignments, and other instruments, and to perform all acts, as may be reasonably requested by the Company to secure, perfect, register, maintain, and enforce the Company's rights in Company Intellectual Property, both during and after the Founder's engagement with the Company. The Founder hereby irrevocably appoints the Company and its officers as the Founder's attorney-in-fact to execute any such documents on the Founder's behalf if the Founder fails or refuses to do so.

### 2.6 Records and Disclosure
The Founder agrees to keep complete and accurate records of all Inventions and Developments and to disclose promptly to the Company all such Inventions and Developments.

### 3.1 Prior Inventions Not Assigned

### 3.2 State Law Exceptions
This Agreement does not require assignment of any Invention that qualifies for protection under applicable state law as an invention developed entirely on the Founder's own time without using Company equipment, supplies, facilities, or Confidential Information, unless the Invention:

(a) relates to the Company's business or actual or demonstrably anticipated research or development; or

(b) results from any work performed by the Founder for the Company.

Applicable state law exceptions are set forth in the State Law Addendum attached hereto.

## ARTICLE 4 — CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

### 4.1 Protection of Confidential Information
The Founder agrees to hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and not to disclose, publish, or otherwise reveal any Confidential Information to any third party, except as required in the performance of the Founder's duties to the Company or as authorized in writing by the Company.

### 4.2 Use Restrictions
The Founder agrees to use Confidential Information only for the benefit of the Company and in the performance of the Founder's duties to the Company, and not for any personal benefit or the benefit of any third party.

### 4.3 Third-Party Confidential Information
The Founder agrees not to improperly use, disclose, or induce the Company to use any confidential information or trade secrets of any former employer or other third party.

### 4.4 Return of Materials
Upon termination of the Founder's engagement with the Company, or upon the Company's request, the Founder agrees to return to the Company all documents, materials, and property containing or relating to Confidential Information, and to delete or destroy all electronic copies in the Founder's possession or control.

### 4.5 Exceptions
Confidential Information does not include information that:

(a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Founder;

(b) was rightfully in the Founder's possession prior to disclosure by the Company;

(c) is rightfully obtained by the Founder from a third party without restriction on disclosure;

(d) is independently developed by the Founder without use of Confidential Information; or

(e) is required to be disclosed by law, provided the Founder gives the Company prompt notice and cooperates with any efforts to obtain protective treatment.

### 4.6 Whistleblower Protection
Nothing in this Agreement prohibits the Founder from reporting possible violations of law to any governmental agency or entity, or from making disclosures protected under whistleblower provisions of applicable law. The Founder is not required to notify the Company of any such report or disclosure.

### 4.7 Defend Trade Secrets Act Notice
Pursuant to 18 U.S.C. § 1833(b):

> An individual shall not be held criminally or civilly liable under any Federal or State trade secret law for the disclosure of a trade secret that is made in confidence to a Federal, State, or local government official, either directly or indirectly, or to an attorney, solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law. An individual shall not be held criminally or civilly liable under any Federal or State trade secret law for the disclosure of a trade secret that is made in a complaint or other document filed in a lawsuit or other proceeding, if such filing is made under seal.

### 5.2 Non-Solicitation of Customers and Business Partners
During the Founder's engagement with the Company and for a period of [twelve (12) / twenty-four (24)] months following termination, the Founder agrees not to directly or indirectly solicit any customer, client, supplier, or business partner of the Company to terminate or reduce their business relationship with the Company.

## ARTICLE 6 — NON-COMPETITION

### 6.1 Non-Competition Covenant
[Note: Non-competition provisions are subject to significant variation in enforceability across states. See State Law Addendum.]

During the Founder's engagement with the Company and for a period of [twelve (12) / twenty-four (24)] months following termination, the Founder agrees not to directly or indirectly:

(a) engage in any Competing Business as an owner, partner, shareholder (except as a passive holder of not more than 2% of a publicly traded company), officer, director, employee, consultant, or agent;

(b) provide services to any Competing Business that are similar to the services provided by the Founder to the Company;

(c) assist any person or entity in engaging in any Competing Business.

### 6.2 Definition of Competing Business
"Competing Business" means any business that develops, produces, markets, or sells products or services that are competitive with the products or services of the Company.

### 6.3 Geographic Scope
The restrictions in this Article 6 shall apply in all geographic areas where the Company conducts business or has plans to conduct business as of the date of termination.

### 6.4 State Law Modifications
The enforceability and scope of non-competition covenants varies significantly by state. The provisions of this Article 6 are subject to modification as set forth in the State Law Addendum.

This Agreement shall become effective on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until terminated by either party.
